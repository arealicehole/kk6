## CAROUSEL SPECIFICATIONS

**Technical Requirements:**
- **Number of Cards:** 5
- **Image Size:** 1080 x 1080 pixels (square) for each card
- **File Format:** JPG or PNG
- **File Size:** Under 30MB per image
- **Total Carousel Size:** Under 150MB
- **Placements:** Instagram Feed, Facebook Feed (Carousel not available in Stories)

**Carousel Behavior:**
- Users swipe left to advance through cards (mobile)
- Users click arrows to advance (desktop)
- Each card can have unique headline and description
- All cards link to same destination URL

## DESIGN GUIDELINES

### Typography
**Headline Font (on images):**
- Sans-serif, bold, high contrast
- Recommended: Montserrat Bold, Raleway Bold, Bebas Neue
- Size: 80-120pt (depending on text length)

**Body Text Font:**
- Sans-serif, medium weight, easy to read
- Recommended: Open Sans, Lato, Source Sans Pro
- Size: 40-60pt

**Accent Text (URLs, small details):**
- Same as body font or complementary serif
- Size: 30-40pt

### Color Specifications
**Text Colors:**
- Primary: White (#FFFFFF) with black drop shadow (40% opacity, 4px blur)
- Secondary: Cream/Off-white (#F5F5DC) for variation
- Accent: Gold (#FFD700) for highlights

**Background Colors (if using solid backgrounds):**
- Deep Red: #8B0000
- Forest Green: #0B6623
- Navy Blue: #1A237E (for charity card)

### Spacing & Layout
- Maintain 80px margin from edges (10% on each side)
- Center-align most text for mobile readability
- Leave breathing room (don't overcrowd images with text)
- Text should cover no more than 40% of image area

## SUCCESS CRITERIA

**This carousel will be successful if:**
- CTR (Click-Through Rate) > 1.2%
- CPC (Cost Per Click) < $0.55
- Swipe-through rate > 40% (users view 3+ cards)
- Engagement (likes, shares, comments) > 2% of impressions
- Positive comments mentioning charity/mission
- Lower CPC than Version B if hypothesis holds

**Hypothesis:**
Charity-forward carousel allows for deeper storytelling than video, engaging detail-oriented audiences who want to understand the impact. Sequential narrative builds trust and motivates action from mission-aligned attendees.

---

## A/B TEST COMPARISON NOTES

**Carousel vs Video (Version A):**
- **Carousel Pros:** More information, user-controlled pace, works on slow connections
- **Carousel Cons:** Requires engagement (swiping), less "snackable" than video
- **Video Pros:** Passive consumption, higher engagement, better for Stories placement
- **Video Cons:** Requires good connection, auto-plays can annoy users

**Expected Performance:**
- Carousel may have lower CTR but higher quality leads (people who engaged deeply)
- Video will likely have higher overall reach and volume
- Carousel better for retargeting (people who engaged before)
